The Visibility Gap

Satellites see the surface.
Bleaching starts below it.

Every existing satellite and surface monitoring system measures the top few metres of ocean. Mesophotic reefs — the ecosystems at 30 to 200 metres depth — are entirely invisible to them. Yet this is where thermal stress first manifests, weeks to months before any surface indicator appears.

🛰

Satellite ceiling: ~5 metres

Sea surface temperature sensors have zero penetration below the photic zone. No remote sensing tool currently monitors mesophotic conditions.

📉

$375B at risk

Coral reefs support over one billion livelihoods and generate $375 billion in annual economic output. Current monitoring misses the leading indicator of their collapse.

⏱

Months of advance signal

Our data shows a bleaching precursor signal at depth that precedes surface-visible events. This window is where intervention, insurance, and investment decisions must be made.
